Description

Doro Wot is a traditional Ethiopian chicken stew made with chicken, onions, garlic, ginger, and a blend of spices including berbere.
It is often served with injera, a sourdough flatbread, and is known for its rich, spicy flavor and deep red color.
Doro Wot is a staple in Ethiopian cuisine and is commonly enjoyed during special occasions and holidays.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (cut into pieces)
  • Onions (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ginger (grated)
  • Berbere spice mix
  • Tomato paste
  • Chicken broth or water
  • Hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil or niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter)

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil or niter kibbeh over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are golden brown and caramelized.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the berbere spice mix and tomato paste, stirring well to combine.
  5. Add the chicken pieces to the pot, coating them with the spice mixture.
  6. Pour in chicken broth or water to cover the chicken, then bring to a simmer.
  7. Cover and cook on low heat for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  8. If using, add hard-boiled eggs during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.
  10. Serve hot with injera or rice.
